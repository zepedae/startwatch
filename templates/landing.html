An effort-based time-management tool
Checking off to-do list items is important, but work that involves a lot of troubleshooting, creativity, or other forms of uncertainty, unforseen circumstances can make this difficult. Despite your considerable effort, these unchecked lists can create a sense of failure that can reduce motivation or increasing overworking - causing you to miss out on the power of "stepping away" for creative problem solving.

Startwatch solves this problem by using effort-based goals to help you manage your productivity across different projects. After setting a time-based productivity goal for a project (e.g., 2 hours/day), users can create a "watch" to record productivity for different projects each day. Productivity time can then be visualized so users can evaluate their productivity across time.

Achieving productivity goals using this effort-based strategy leaves you with a feeling of accomplishment even on the days when your to-do list doesn't change much. On days when your tasks are completed before your time goal is up, the strategy has the added bonus of encouraging you to work on those less time-sensitive, easy to avoid tasks.
• Use in conjunction with a time-based productivity goal and to-do list every day
• On days you don't finish your to-do list you know you've made the effort
• On days you finish your to-do list before your time goal, spend the extra time working on things you avoid adding to or push to the bottom of your to-do list
